## Series thesis

The series maps connective tissue architectures, energy meridians, fascial force-transmission lines, tensegrity geometries, and frequency-body correspondences.

The aesthetic directive:

> Historical precision meets contemporary vision. What Vesalius did for muscles and bones, this series does for the invisible structures that hold everything together.

| Technique | Weight | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| Primary outlines | 0.15mm–0.8mm variable | major boundaries, depth hierarchy, confident structural marks |

## Background rule

Backgrounds are always aged parchment, never pure white.

Recommended gradient:

```text
#F5F0E6 → #E8DCC8
```

## Composition rules

- Central specimen occupies 60–70% of the plate area.
- Callout lines radiate outward from specimen to margin annotations.
- Callouts use small circles or fine arrows at the specimen end.
- Callout lines should not cross.
- Margin annotations use small serif italic.
- Plate number appears upper-left in Roman numerals: `PLATE IV`.
- Series title appears along bottom margin: `ANATOMICA INVISIBILIA`.
- Scale bars are included when anatomically meaningful.

## Typography

| Element | Typeface | Size | Style | Color |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Primary labels | Georgia or Garamond | 9pt | small caps | Anatomical Red or dark sepia |
| Secondary annotations | Georgia | 7pt | italic | dark sepia |
| Plate title | Georgia | 14pt | regular, centered | dark sepia |
| Latin/scientific terms | Georgia | contextual | italic | contextual |
| Common names | Georgia | contextual | regular | contextual |

Critical rule: never use pure black for plate typography. Use Anatomical Red `#8B3A3A` or dark sepia `#3A2A1A`.

## Completed plate catalog

### Plate I — Fascial Architecture Composite

Cross-section of layered fascia as geological stratigraphy. Includes superficial, deep, and visceral fascia; collagen bundles; elastin networks; ground substance; hyaluronic acid; blood vessels; fasciacytes.

Dominant colors: Fascia Gold, Ground Substance Amber, Anatomical Red.

### Plate II — Meridian Pathway Bloom

Meditative human figure with botanical flowering along heart, liver, and lung meridian lines. Acupuncture points appear as buds; organ connections as root systems.

Dominant colors: Meridian Cyan, Living Green, Nerve Blue.

### Plate III — Fascia-Nerve Interface

Microscopic view of nerve fibers intermingling with fascia layers. Includes Pacinian corpuscles, Ruffini endings, free nerve endings, myelinated and unmyelinated nerve fibers.

Dominant colors: Nerve Blue, Fascia Gold, Anatomical Red.

### Plate IV — Tensegrity Cell Architecture

Single cell rendered as tensegrity structure: actin tension cables, microtubule compression struts, integrin anchors, focal adhesions, nucleus suspended in tension network.

Dominant colors: Nerve Blue, Fascia Gold, Visceral Purple.

### Plate V — Extracellular Matrix Landscape

ECM as microscopic terrain with collagen formations, proteoglycan crystals, fibroblasts, laminin sheets, and fibronectin bridges.

Dominant colors: Fascia Gold, Ground Substance Amber, Bone White.

### Plate VI — Ground Substance — The Liquid Crystal

Split plate showing gel-to-sol thixotropic transition. Includes hyaluronic acid chains, proteoglycans, GAG branches, hydration shells, and streaming-potential indicators.

Dominant colors: Ground Substance Amber, Meridian Cyan, Frequency Violet.
