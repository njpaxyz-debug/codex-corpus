## Core Thesis

Mechanical force becomes cellular information through receptors, cytoskeleton, and signaling cascades.

## Biological Ground

Mechanotransduction is the conversion of pressure, stretch, vibration, and mechanical loading into biological signal. In the Codex it links fascia, receptors, cytoskeleton, integrins, and cellular response.

This entry anchors the bridge between somatic practice and measurable tissue response. It also provides the most important scientific boundary: feeling a response does not automatically prove a claimed therapeutic outcome.

## Technological Mirror

Piezoelectric sensor / transducer / signal converter.

The body is interpreted as a layered signal-processing system in which contact and vibration are translated into electrical, chemical, and perceptual events.

## Practice Gate

40 Hz orientation:

1. Apply gentle palm pressure or soft vibration to a comfortable region.
2. Keep the duration short.
3. Stop stimulation.
4. Wait in stillness.
5. Compare the before/after sensation without forcing interpretation.
