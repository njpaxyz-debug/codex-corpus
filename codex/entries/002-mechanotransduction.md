# 002 — Mechanotransduction: How Touch Becomes Signal

## Core Thesis

Mechanical force becomes cellular information through receptors, cytoskeleton, and signaling cascades.

## Biological Ground

Mechanotransduction frames touch and mechanical loading as input that cells can interpret and convert into biochemical signals.

In the Codex framing, this entry supports a careful translation rule: practice logs report phenomenology (what is observed in self-sensation), while biochemical or cellular effects remain part of a biophysical model unless supported by stronger evidence.

## Technological Mirror

Signal-processing pipeline:

- force input
- receptor/interface detection
- cytoskeletal transmission
- signaling cascade output

## Practice Gate

40 Hz orientation practice:

1. Apply brief, gentle stimulation (for example, palm contact with soft vibration, or an externally generated gentle 40 Hz tone if available).
2. Pause and observe differences in sensation.
3. Record self-observation without overclaiming cellular outcomes.
