## Core Thesis

Mechanical stress can generate electrical charge in collagen-rich and mineralized tissues, making the body’s structural matrix also a signal-bearing material.

## Biological Ground

Piezoelectricity refers to electrical charge generated by mechanical deformation in certain materials. Bone and connective tissue contain ordered molecular structures, including collagen and mineral components, that have been studied for stress-generated electrical potentials.

Within the Codex, this entry supports a careful bridge:

- mechanical loading influences tissue adaptation;
- collagen-rich matrices can participate in electrical phenomena;
- bone remodeling is responsive to mechanical force;
- sound/vibration practices should be framed as exploratory or practice-based unless validated by specific evidence.

This entry is especially important for claim discipline. Piezoelectricity is a real physical concept, but broad therapeutic claims require separate evidence.

## Technological Mirror

Quartz oscillator / piezo sensor / pressure-to-voltage transducer.

In a quartz watch, a crystal converts electrical/mechanical relationships into stable timekeeping. In tissue, the mirror is less a device and more a material principle: pressure and structure can produce signal.

## Practice Gate

Load-and-listen protocol:

1. Stand with feet evenly grounded.
2. Shift weight slowly from heel to forefoot.
3. Avoid maximal effort.
4. Notice pressure, vibration, and tiny oscillations through bones and fascia.
5. Treat sensation as observation, not proof of electrical effect.
