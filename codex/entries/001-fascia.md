# 001 — Fascia: The Body's Undernet

## Core Thesis

Fascia is the continuous tensional network that gives every other system its shape, its address, and its voice.

## Biological Ground

Fascia is treated in the Codex as the body's undernet: a continuous three-dimensional connective tissue matrix extending from superficial layers beneath the skin into deeper muscular, visceral, vascular, neural, and spinal compartments.

The source emphasizes four fundamental categories:

- superficial fascia
- deep fascia
- visceral fascia
- spinal / thoracolumbar fascial structures

Key material properties named in the source include collagen architecture, elastin recoil, ground substance, hyaluronic acid, thixotropy, viscoelasticity, piezoelectricity, and rich innervation.

## Technological Mirror

Infrastructure layer / mesh network / undernet.

Fascia is interpreted as the biological equivalent of a distributed support-and-signal layer: the system underneath the systems most people notice first.

## Practice Gate

Fascial hydration practice:

1. Choose one joint or region.
2. Move slowly in small circles.
3. Keep the breath warm and unforced.
4. Notice sliding, sticking, temperature, and pressure.
5. Do not force release; wait for softening.
